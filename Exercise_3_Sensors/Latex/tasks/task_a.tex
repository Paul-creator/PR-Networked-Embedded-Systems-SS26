\section{Task A: Read Data from the LIS3MDL Magnetic Sensor}

\subsection{a) Calculations:}

A minimal startup sequence for the LIS3MDL consists of: checking the device ID, selecting the full-scale range, enabling continuous conversion mode, and configuring the performance/output data rate. The relevant registers are listed below.

\begin{table}[H]
	\centering
	\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{|>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}X|>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{3cm}|>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{2.4cm}|>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{4.2cm}|}
		\hline
		\textbf{Parameter Description}                                            & \textbf{Value}                        & \textbf{Unit (if any)} & \textbf{Register Address} \\ \hline
		Read device identity (\texttt{WHO\_AM\_I}), expected response             & \texttt{0x3D}                         & --                     & \texttt{0x0F}             \\ \hline
		Configure X/Y performance mode and output data rate (\texttt{CTRL\_REG1}) & e.g.\ \texttt{0x70}                   & --                     & \texttt{0x20}             \\ \hline
		Configure full-scale range (\texttt{CTRL\_REG2})                          & e.g.\ \texttt{0x00} for $\pm 4$~Gauss & Gauss                  & \texttt{0x21}             \\ \hline
		Enable continuous-conversion mode (\texttt{CTRL\_REG3})                   & \texttt{0x00}                         & --                     & \texttt{0x22}             \\ \hline
		Configure Z-axis performance mode (\texttt{CTRL\_REG4})                   & e.g.\ \texttt{0x0C}                   & --                     & \texttt{0x23}             \\ \hline
		Optional: block data update / endianness (\texttt{CTRL\_REG5})            & depending on implementation           & --                     & \texttt{0x24}             \\ \hline
	\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

Meaning of the most important commands:
\begin{itemize}
	\item \texttt{WHO\_AM\_I} verifies that the correct sensor is connected.
	\item \texttt{CTRL\_REG1} sets output data rate and X/Y operating mode.
	\item \texttt{CTRL\_REG2} selects the measurement range (\(\pm 4\), \(\pm 8\), \(\pm 12\), \(\pm 16\)~Gauss).
	\item \texttt{CTRL\_REG3} selects the operating mode; \texttt{0x00} means continuous measurement.
	\item \texttt{CTRL\_REG4} sets the Z-axis operating mode.
\end{itemize}

\textit{How is the magnetic value of each axis calculated from the involved registers? Explain!}

Each axis value is stored in two 8-bit registers: one low byte and one high byte. These two bytes are combined into one signed 16-bit value in two's complement representation:
\[
	M_{x,\mathrm{raw}} = (\texttt{OUT\_X\_H} \ll 8) \; | \; \texttt{OUT\_X\_L}
\]
\[
	M_{y,\mathrm{raw}} = (\texttt{OUT\_Y\_H} \ll 8) \; | \; \texttt{OUT\_Y\_L}
\]
\[
	M_{z,\mathrm{raw}} = (\texttt{OUT\_Z\_H} \ll 8) \; | \; \texttt{OUT\_Z\_L}
\]

After combining, the result must be interpreted as a signed 16-bit integer:
\[
	M_{\mathrm{raw}} \in [-32768,\,32767]
\]

The conversion into physical magnetic flux density depends on the configured full-scale range. For the LIS3MDL, the sensitivities are:

\[
	\begin{array}{c|c}
		\text{Range}        & \text{Sensitivity}    \\ \hline
		\pm 4~\text{Gauss}  & 6842~\text{LSB/Gauss} \\
		\pm 8~\text{Gauss}  & 3421~\text{LSB/Gauss} \\
		\pm 12~\text{Gauss} & 2281~\text{LSB/Gauss} \\
		\pm 16~\text{Gauss} & 1711~\text{LSB/Gauss}
	\end{array}
\]

Thus, for a selected sensitivity \(S\), the magnetic field value is:
\[
	M_{\mathrm{Gauss}} = \frac{M_{\mathrm{raw}}}{S}
\]

Example for the default range \(\pm 4\)~Gauss:
\[
	M_{x,\mathrm{Gauss}} = \frac{M_{x,\mathrm{raw}}}{6842}, \qquad
	M_{y,\mathrm{Gauss}} = \frac{M_{y,\mathrm{raw}}}{6842}, \qquad
	M_{z,\mathrm{Gauss}} = \frac{M_{z,\mathrm{raw}}}{6842}
\]

Therefore, the program must:
\begin{enumerate}
	\item read two bytes per axis via I\textsuperscript{2}C,
	\item combine them to one signed 16-bit value,
	\item divide by the sensitivity corresponding to the configured full-scale range,
	\item print the result via UART.
\end{enumerate}
