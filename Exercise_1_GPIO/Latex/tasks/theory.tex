\subsection{a) What does the abbreviation GPIO mean?}
GPIO stands for \textbf{General Purpose Input/Output}. It refers to programmable digital pins on a microcontroller or processor that can be configured either as an input or as an output.

\subsection{b) What functionality do GPIOs have and what are they used for?}
GPIO pins allow a microcontroller to interact with external electronic components.

Main functionalities:
\begin{itemize}
	\item \textbf{Input mode:} The pin reads a digital signal (HIGH or LOW) from external devices such as buttons, switches, or sensors.
	\item \textbf{Output mode:} The pin outputs a digital signal (HIGH or LOW) to control external components such as LEDs, relays, or motors.
\end{itemize}

GPIOs are therefore used for general digital interfacing between the microcontroller and external hardware.

\subsection{c) What is a duty cycle? How do you calculate it?}
The \textbf{duty cycle} describes the proportion of time a periodic signal stays in the HIGH state within one period.

It is defined as the ratio between the active time \(T_{on}\) and the total period \(T\):

\[
	D = \frac{T_{on}}{T}
\]

\begin{itemize}
	\item \(T_{on}\) = time the signal is HIGH
	\item \(T\) = total period of the signal
\end{itemize}
